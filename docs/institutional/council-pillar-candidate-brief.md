# Council-pillar candidate brief — template

**Audience:** five individuals, one per pillar
(judicial / civil society / academic / technical / religious),
recruited by the architect to constitute the VIGIL APEX Governance
Council under §22 of the v5.1 commercial agreement.
**Sender:** Junior Thuram Nana, Sovereign Architect, VIGIL APEX SAS,
on behalf of the company.
**Channel:** Bespoke per-candidate. Email to start; phone follow-up
within 5 business days; in-person meeting before any signature.
**No group send under any circumstance.** A copy of this brief
arriving in two candidates' inboxes simultaneously breaks pillar
independence and is considered a recruitment failure.
**Bilingual:** FR primary; EN companion. The architect picks the
language by candidate.

## Architect-only handling notes

### Sequencing
1. Identify five candidates. The architect maintains the candidate
   list in `personal/council-candidates.md` (gitignored). Three
   names per pillar minimum so a decline at any pillar doesn't
   block the others.
2. Order the brief by **easiest pillar first**. The civil society
   pillar typically replies fastest; the judicial pillar slowest.
3. **One pillar at a time** until you have at least one yes.
   Premature parallel sends create awkwardness if multiple
   candidates per pillar say yes.
4. Pass the briefed candidate to **counsel** for the candidacy
   dossier. The architect's job is the brief; counsel's job is the
   contract.

### Risk controls
- **Never** include another pillar's candidate in a forwarded
  message. Pillar independence is structural.
- **Never** name a sitting public official in the brief without
  their separately-acquired consent — sending a "we'd like you,
  Magistrate X" letter that ends up archived by Magistrate X's
  clerk is a leak risk and a procedural foul.
- **Audit row** on every send: `action = institutional.send.council-pillar.<pillar>`,
  payload includes the candidate's pillar but **NOT** their name
  (the name lives in the architect's gitignored archive).

### Compensation paragraph (§5.1)
Calibrate by pillar. As of 2026, a defensible range:
- Civil society / academic / religious: CFA 3 M – CFA 5 M annual
  (€4,500 – €7,500 equivalent).
- Judicial / technical: CFA 5 M – CFA 8 M annual.
The amount must be high enough to compensate the time + reputational
risk and low enough that no candidate would shape a vote for the
indemnity. Counsel reviews the final number.

### Reply window
- Civil society pillar: 1–2 weeks typical.
- Academic pillar: 2–4 weeks.
- Religious pillar: 2–4 weeks; protocol varies by denomination.
- Technical pillar: 1–2 weeks.
- Judicial pillar: 4–8 weeks; magistrate calendars are dense.

If a candidate has not replied within the upper bound + 2 weeks, a
gentle phone follow-up is appropriate. Do not re-send the brief.

### What to do if **all** candidates in a pillar decline
EXEC §10.4 covers this. Per EXEC, the architect (a) widens the
candidate pool by region and profession, (b) lowers the
"recognition tier" of the candidates by one notch (e.g. from
"national-level magistrate" to "appellate-level magistrate"), and
(c) consults the seated council on whether the pillar can be
temporarily filled with an interim member. The platform must reach
**at least 3 active members across distinct pillars** before any
escalation vote can be held under SRD §22.5.
